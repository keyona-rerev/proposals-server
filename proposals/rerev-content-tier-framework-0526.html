The three-tier content stack

Most founder content fails not because of bad writing, but because of missing structure. Every post is trying to do too many jobs at once. This framework gives each piece of content one job, and connects them into a system that compounds.

The core insight: The tiers are directional. You earn the right to publish Tier 2 by having Tier 1 on the ground. You earn the right to have Tier 1 land by having a Tier 3 engine that gives people a reason to keep coming back. Build from the bottom up.

Tier 1 Mid-Funnel

Direct company content designed for people who are already evaluating you. Thesis explainers, use case walkthroughs, before and after stories. This content lives on your website and gets scheduled on LinkedIn.

Tier 2 Market Signal

External events and data translated through your thesis. Reactive in timing, strategic in framing. You are not sharing news, you are interpreting it as someone with standing. That standing comes from Tier 3 existing first.

Tier 3 Flagship Series

A named, scheduled, owned content engine that runs independently of what your company is doing week to week. Not an announcement. Not a thought for the day. A publication. This is the tier most founders never build, and the one that makes everything else compound.

Named series Recurring format Fixed schedule Owned distribution
Widest reach, builds over time

How the tiers feed each other

The stack is not just vertical. The tiers are in constant conversation. One piece of work can produce content across all three tiers in the same week.

Tier 3 → Tier 1

A flagship series episode becomes the source material for a direct product explainer or thesis piece the following week.

Tier 3 → Tier 2

A series episode generates anchoring statistics and frameworks that power months of market signal commentary on LinkedIn.

Tier 2 → Tier 1

A market signal post interpreting a new report creates natural demand for the product page that shows how you are positioned to act on it.

The tier most founders skip

Everyone has some version of Tier 1. Most founders have some version of Tier 2, even if unintentional. Almost nobody has Tier 3.

The flagship series is the thing that makes content compound. It is the gravitational center that gives individual posts context and gives the audience a reason to follow you specifically rather than whoever else is posting about your category. Without it, content never builds on itself. There is no engine, just a series of individual posts with no anchor.

Build Tier 1 messaging first, because it is the foundation everything references. Stand up the Tier 3 series before you start worrying about reach. Tier 2 will emerge naturally once both are in place.
